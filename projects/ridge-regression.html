Adaptive Time Series Forecasting with Rolling Ridge Regression

Medium Article · ^GSPC (2014–2024)

Overview

Rolling-window Ridge Regression applied to ^GSPC (S&P 500) using usa_data for 2014–2024. A simple yet powerful setup for financial time series forecasts — combining deterministic trends with stochastic residual adjustments.

Key Takeaways

  • Long-term trends — Ridge Regression captures general deterministic trends via regularization, linearity, and multicollinearity control
  • Stochastic residuals — Residual adjustments capture randomness and short-term deviations, enriching predictions beyond pure linear trends
  • Short-term limitations — As a linear method, Ridge Regression struggles with nonlinear relationships and high volatility in financial series
  • Trade-off — Ideally suited for broad trends; Neural Networks or Tree-Based Models may perform better for short-term or highly volatile regimes

Conclusion

The rolling-window approach with Ridge Regression forms a good fit for modeling deterministic trends over time. The trade-off between simplicity and sophistication makes it ideal for capturing broad trends in financial data, while recognizing that more sophisticated methods are required for shorter horizons or volatile conditions.
